# Project Roadmap

This roadmap stays wide on purpose. The best way to keep a wide roadmap useful is to place larger projects after the chapters that give you the right prerequisites.

## Core milestone projects

- After Chapter 10: Build a simple C static library with headers, tests, and a Makefile.
- After Chapter 18 or 20: Build a polished GUI desktop app in GTK4.
- After Chapter 25: Build a reusable Rust library crate.
- After Chapter 29: Build a practical Rust project such as a CLI tool or terminal game.
- After Chapter 36: Build a simple backend using raw sockets or a TCP chat server.
- After Chapter 43: Build one ambitious systems project such as a toy compiler, tiny GUI library, mini shell, or small game framework.
- After Chapter 48: Build a browser game in modern JavaScript.
- After Chapter 51 or 53: Build a simple backend API in Node.js or Rust Axum.
- After Chapter 54: Build a full-stack CRUD app with a database.
- After Chapter 56 or 58: Build a real Flutter app with persistence and stronger architecture.
- After Chapter 59: Deploy and operate one of your backend or full-stack projects.
- After Chapter 60: Build a flagship capstone that combines multiple tracks.

## Recommended big-project ideas

- Simple compiler: best after Chapter 43 as a stretch systems project.
- Simple library: best after Chapter 10 in C and after Chapter 25 in Rust.
- Simple game: best after Chapter 29 in Rust or Chapter 48 in JavaScript.
- Simple GUI library: best after Chapter 43 as an advanced stretch project.
- Simple GUI app: best after Chapter 18 or Chapter 20.
- Simple backend: best after Chapter 36, 51, or 53 depending on the stack you want.
- Full-stack web app: best after Chapter 54.
- Mobile app: best after Chapter 56 or 58.
- Linux service: best after Chapter 37.
- Custom distro experiment: best after Chapter 39.

## Recommendation

Yes, add these projects after the relevant chapters instead of placing them all at the very end.

Why this works better:

- You get a reward loop after each learning block.
- You turn theory into proof-of-skill while the concepts are still fresh.
- You build a portfolio gradually instead of waiting until the course is finished.
- You discover weak spots earlier, which makes later chapters easier.
